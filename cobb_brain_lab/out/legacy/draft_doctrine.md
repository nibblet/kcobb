# Draft Doctrine (from clusters)

Consolidate into 12–20 core principles.

## opportunity_effectively public_aptitude tests

- If an opportunity requires a commitment you are unwilling to sustain, decline it rather than participate half-heartedly.
- Consistent intellectual curiosity and exposure to literature expand students' horizons in limited-opportunity settings.
- When uncertain about career direction, take aptitude tests and consult mentors before deciding.
- Private, firm encouragement motivates improvement more effectively than public scolding.
- When a student underperforms, address them privately and set a higher expectation instead of immediately escalating to caregivers.

_Stories:_ P1_S01, P1_S02, P1_S03, P1_S05

## learning_community activities_participation

- Use participation in community activities as low-risk opportunities for learning and growth.
- Hands-on community activities and local institutions provide practical learning opportunities.
- Use structured milestones and visible recognition to motivate progress and learning.
- Implement rigorous, repeatable practice rituals to create durable learning (e.g., annual memorization exercises).
- Be honest with yourself about willingness to invest time; declining commitments may be preferable to half-hearted participation.

_Stories:_ P1_S01, P1_S03, P1_S04, P1_S05

## discipline_work_require

- Require completion of substantive work before awarding recognition to build discipline.
- Require genuine effort before granting rewards to instill discipline and competence.
- Regret follows neglected natural gifts when discipline is lacking.
- Value community and hard work as central virtues.

_Stories:_ P1_S01, P1_S04, P1_S05

## development_home_home school

- Early influences (home, school, church) are foundational to character development.
- Anchor development efforts in the combined influence of home, school, and faith communities.
- Monitor each person's development individually to tailor guidance and accountability.

_Stories:_ P1_S01, P1_S04

## memorials_memorials scholarships_scholarships

- When honoring a mentor, mobilize peers and alumni to fund tangible memorials and scholarships to continue their impact.
- Communities can preserve and extend a leader's legacy through collective action (memorials and scholarships).
- Create clear ranks, badges, and score-keeping to turn progress into tangible, motivating milestones.

_Stories:_ P1_S03, P1_S04

## mentor_create_skill gaps

- Mentor demonstration and support can compensate for formal-skill gaps and create opportunities.
- When written music is overwhelming, learn by listening and rehearse with a knowledgeable mentor to create an audition-quality performance.

_Stories:_ P1_S05

## skills_durable skills_opportunity learn

- Extracurricular activities build skills, confidence, and social bonds.
- Adversity can be an opportunity to learn durable skills.

_Stories:_ P1_S02

## term_long term_long

- Consistent mentorship during formative years shapes long-term character and choices.
- Use recovery or downtime to practice and acquire skills that yield long-term benefits.

_Stories:_ P1_S02, P1_S04

## high_lasting_disciplined practice

- High standards and disciplined practice produce lasting knowledge and character.
- High-quality teaching produces lasting advantage and prepares for future success.

_Stories:_ P1_S02, P1_S03

## delegation_delegation skills_develop delegation

- If volunteers won't contribute, be prepared to complete critical tasks yourself but recognize and plan to develop delegation skills later.
- Leaders must develop delegation skills rather than assuming excessive responsibility.

_Stories:_ P1_S02

## model_behaviors_expect

- Leaders should model the behaviors they expect from others.
- Model desired behavior by consistently participating and visibly following the same standards as the group.

_Stories:_ P1_S04

## engagement_technical_enjoyment technical

- Lifelong engagement can come from enjoyment even without technical mastery.
- Use allotted practice time to focus on personally motivating material (play by ear) to maintain engagement when technical drills are aversive.

_Stories:_ P1_S05
