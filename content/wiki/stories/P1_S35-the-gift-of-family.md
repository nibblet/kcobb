The Gift of Family
“ An ounce of blood is worth more than a pound of friendship.” —Spanish proverb
As I have reflected on my life in developing these memoirs, I have come to realize that family has been the bedrock pillar supporting my existence for the overwhelming majority of my lifetime. Much has been wri tten about family values and relationships, and I
suppose there is not much wisdom for me to add to the library. I will simply devote a few words describing the members of our family and the loving relationship we have developed.
Our family has always been close in spirit, yet sca ttered geographically. Therefore, unlike many families we know who live near one another and spend large chunks of their time together,
our face-to-face contact over the years has been sporadic. However,
distance has not seemed to us to be a stumbling block for our love and affection for one another.
Physical separation from our children began when Paul finished high school and le ft for the U.S. Air Force Academy, two thousand miles away from our home in Fort Lauderdale. Dot and I still remember seeing him walk down that ramp to the airplane. A while later, John left for college at Lehigh University and Mark at James
Madison University. They never returned home to live and have been scattered around the country ever since.
Paul’s first Air Force base after flight school was in Tucson,
Arizona. It was there he met a beautiful young woman named Dawn
Garrison. They fell in love and married there in Tucson. Dawn had dozens of wonderful qualities and proved to be the perfect mate for
Paul. T ogether they began to build their own family and have lived in six di fferent locations since their wedding. Having retired after twenty-three years as a pilot in the Air Force, Paul is now working as a project manager for Google. Dawn is a terrific, diversified woman.
Of all her a ttributes, I think I most admire how she took charge of their affairs and managed their family when Paul was deployed on several extended tours in the Middle East. She was a real trooper during those times. She is a cheerful, energetic and loving person who is a wonderful wife and mother. We love Dawn!
Miranda was the first of our grandchildren, born January 24,
1995. What a joy it has been to watch her grow and develop over the past twenty years. She has always been a good student and hard worker. She was also an excellent athlete in high school. She finished high school at the top of her large senior class and chose the University of Alabama for her college education. T oday, she is a junior and has been recognized as one of the most outstanding students on that large campus. She has truly developed into a most distinguished young woman. Dot and I love the fact that she stays in regular touch with us and visits us here in Florida from time to time.
Jordan was the next addition to the family, born May 18, 1998.
From the get-go, it was clear that Jordan might be destined to be most precocious of the Cobb family. She is naturally extremely bright and has always been at the top of her class. She has scored off the charts on all of the academic measuring sticks. She’s now a senior in high school and is in the process of choosing from a large sample of elite universities that all undoubtedly will be anxious to admit her. Currently she is interested in a career in nursing. Aside from her unique intellect, she is a charming and energetic young woman who is just as delightful to us and as fun to be around as her older sister.
She seems to have plucked the most important virtues of both her mother and father in the gene auction.
We believe that both Miranda and Jordan have actually benefitted from moving every three or four years. Changing venues so many times has made them more adaptable, independent and self-confident young women, in our view, than they might have been otherwise.
John, after working in Pennsylvania and Florida for a short while,
moved to Chicago, where he has settled for the past fifteen years. He now is a senior executive with Chicago-based Ventas Corporation.
He was most fortunate to meet Sarah Meier, who was living in the same apartment building when he arrived there. What a discovery for John! They married and began their lives together, both working in professional careers. Sarah initially worked as a consultant for
PricewaterhouseCoopers and later moved to Goldman Sachs. After receiving her MBA from Northwestern University, she served for a few years as the CEO of a large non-pro fit organization there in
Chicago. She is a delightful young woman, with a heart of gold, and she has been a remarkably wonderful mate for John. Sarah quickly became a certified member of the Cobb clan!
Avery was the next gi ft to the Cobb family. She was born in
Chicago on January 27, 2007. It’s been so much fun to watch Avery grow and develop in their urban environment over the past eight years. John and Sarah have been fabulous parents and have exposed
Avery to a world of diversity. She now is a student at the prestigious
Latin School and is performing exceptionally well academically.
Avery is an exceptionally bright child with a loving disposition. She is also quite skilled in a variety of activities, including ice skating,
gymnastics, dance and sailing. Dot and I have thoroughly enjoyed the opportunities we have had to visit with her from time to time in
Chicago and their weekend place in Lake Geneva, Wisconsin. Avery is a winner, no doubt about it!
Mark began his career with Enterprise Rent A Car in Washington,
D.C., later transferring to Colorado to live out his outdoor dreams.
After a while, he shi fted employment to the online education industry, where he still works as an executive. In the process, he had the extremely good fortune to stumble upon an extraordinary young woman named Sue Nickerson, who was working in the same company at the time. Mark and Sue were kindred spirits with interests that fit like a glove. They soon found themselves married and building a life together. Like our other daughters-in-law, Sue is a beautiful, smart and loving young woman. She is always cheerful,
upbeat and likeable—a genuine pleasure to be with. Dot and I have enjoyed a wonderful relationship. Sue is a winner, just like the others!
Allison appeared in our lives as our fourth granddaughter on
September 22, 2008. She was a most beautiful child from thevery beginning. T oday, she is in the first grade at school and growing like a weed. Ally, as we all call her, has an indescribable lovely disposition.
She is cute, sweet, upbeat and seemingly always happy. There doesn’t appear to be a single negative bone in her body. I suppose some of her obvious goodness is taken from her mother, who shares many of her lovely traits. Dot and I get a kick out of visiting with Ally on
FaceTime, seeing her perpetual cheerful smiling face and listening to her peppy voice!
And then along comes Ethan Keith Cobb on March 15, 2012.
Our first grandson! I was touched by the selection of his middle name, of course, but even more touched seeing him emerge as all
The entire family celebrating our fiftieth wedding anniversary in the
Caribbean boy, so di fferent from his sister and cousins. Although he’s only three years old now, he is already showing remarkable athletic skills and a keen interest in sports. Perhaps he may be just as precocious athletically as Jordan has been academically. Ethan is an unusually handsome little guy and is an incredible ball of energy. He never stops moving. Ethan is lots of fun to be with!
That rounds out the thirteen members of our immediate family. We are physically spread out from Florida to Washington,
D.C. to Chicago to Denver, but we manage to stay in regular close touch through the beauty of modern communications technology.
Occasionally, we are able to assemble everyone at the same time for a family gathering. The last of these times was a Caribbean cruise celebrating our fiftieth wedding anniversary, a wonderful time together.
Like so much of our lives, Dot and I marvel at how trouble-free our family has been. Everyone is healthy, happy and financially comfortable, and we’ve all been spared so much of the misery and problems that plague so many families. I doubt there is a formula for our happiness. Perhaps it is just the luck of the draw. Whatever the root, the vine is healthy and happy and the fruit is abundant. We are indeed overjoyed to have such a wonderful, loving family.
I am also extremely grateful for my relationship with Sandy,
my terrific sister, who has been a loyal supporter and con fidant for my entire life. I love her and cherish the great relationship we have shared.
I recently saw a Desmond Tutu quote that resonated with me:
“Y ou don’t choose your family. They are God’s gift to you, as you are to them. ” I truly regard my family as an extraordinary gift.
