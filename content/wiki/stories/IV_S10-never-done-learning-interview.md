# Never Done Learning (Interview)

> Keith discusses experimenting with AI — uploading his memoir into ChatGPT to learn about the technology and potentially write a sequel. He reflects that it's never too late to learn new things. The conversation closes with appreciation for Broward County's community spirit and Keith's enduring optimism about the future.

## Full Text

*Transcript edited lightly for clarity and readability; wording and voice preserved.*

**Keith:** I've been thinking about doing a sequel to that book and using it as an experiment to learn more about AI. I uploaded that book into ChatGPT—and now it knows me. I've been experimenting with it. I don't know; it takes a while to do that. But it's never too late to learn new stuff, right? Oh, no—that's who I am.

**Cagnetta:** You're a hard worker. Do you think that came from your parents? Where did that come from?

**Keith:** I think so. I know your dad and mom were hardworking people. They were hardworking people, and they made sure that I was too. They led me in that direction. I tell people I've had a job of some kind since I was 12 years old, so I've always done that. I think my parents were a big influence on me. I speak of this unique microcosm of a community—that was an ethic in that community. People worked, and they studied, and they learned, and they mixed and mingled. That was how that community operated. Some places don't have all of that opportunity. I was very fortunate—I've been enormously fortunate and grateful for that over the years.

**Cagnetta:** Well, we're fortunate to have you as part of our community—and I have hope for Broward, too. I think we do a good job. I'll mention the Broward workshop where I probably ran into you the most—maybe at United Way as well—but I think we do a pretty good job of trying to do that mixing and mingling, trying to get all the people who can help make our community a better place in one room.

**Keith:** The Broward Workshop has been a great catalyst for that over the years. As you know, we've both been members for a long time. They've had some wonderful people associated with it, and they've done some wonderful things in the community. You're absolutely right—it has been a catalyst for the community, and I agree with you. I think things are coming up roses here in Broward County. It's certainly more promising to me than many other places around the country.

**Keith:** For sure.

**Cagnetta:** For sure. Yeah, no—we're very excited. Again, thank you for coming in today; I really appreciate it. I'm going to highly suggest that you somehow get this on Amazon so other people can read it.

**Keith:** Okay.

**Cagnetta:** Keith, thank you for coming in today. I really appreciate it.

**Keith:** You're welcome. I enjoyed it. Thank you so much, buddy.

**Cagnetta:** All right, everybody—episode of Coffee with Cagnetta. We'll catch you next time.

## What This Story Shows

- It's never too late to learn new things — curiosity has no retirement age.
- Experimentation is the best way to learn — try things hands-on rather than just reading about them.
- Stay optimistic about your community's future and contribute to it.

## If You're Thinking About...

- When a new technology emerges, experiment with it directly rather than waiting for others to explain it.
- When considering whether you're too old or too established to try something new, remember: you're not.
