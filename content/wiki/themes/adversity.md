# Adversity

> Stories and principles related to adversity.

## Principles

- High-quality teaching produces lasting advantage and prepares for future success. _(P1_S02)_
- Extracurricular activities build skills, confidence, and social bonds. _(P1_S02)_
- Leaders must develop delegation skills rather than assuming excessive responsibility. _(P1_S02)_
- Adversity can be an opportunity to learn durable skills. _(P1_S02)_
- Lifelong engagement can come from enjoyment even without technical mastery. _(P1_S05)_
- Mentor demonstration and support can compensate for formal-skill gaps and create opportunities. _(P1_S05)_
- Be honest with yourself about willingness to invest time; declining commitments may be preferable to half-hearted participation. _(P1_S05)_
- Regret follows neglected natural gifts when discipline is lacking. _(P1_S05)_
- Lead by example rather than by instruction. _(P1_S06)_
- Maintain calm composure under stress; model steadiness. _(P1_S06)_
