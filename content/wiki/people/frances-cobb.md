## About Frances Cobb

Mary Frances Clements Cobb (1921–2001) was Keith's mother, a woman of exceptional intelligence and industriousness who poured enormous energy into her children's development while struggling with her own unfulfilled ambitions and chronic health problems.

Born into relative poverty in the hill country of Lafayette and Pontotoc counties in north Mississippi, Frances finished high school at rural Toccopola and worked as a beautician in Sarepta before marrying Bayne Cobb in 1940. The couple settled in Calhoun City, where her entire life took place within about thirty miles, mostly on one parcel of property for sixty-one years (P1_S07). She launched a successful home-based seamstress business, becoming the go-to person for difficult sewing projects in town, then worked outside the home at Lunceford's Bargain Store, Chandler's Drug Store, and Beasley Insurance Agency—each job "a step up from the previous one" (P1_S07). She was a strict disciplinarian and taskmaster who engineered a "beehive of activity" for her children, instilling in Keith his work ethic, self-discipline, and fundamental value system (P1_S07). Yet she seemed to suffer from insecurity and "a sort of persecution complex," repeating incessantly that she had always wanted an education but couldn't afford it, and Keith notes that "nothing was good enough, no matter how satisfactory my achievements seemed to me" (P1_S07). Keith remembers her as both his greatest teacher and a source of profound disappointment, acknowledging: "I shall be forever grateful for all of this" (P1_S07).

**Notable moments**

- Taught Keith color matching and sent him to the Ben Franklin store with fabric samples—including a Noxzema container to match dark blue—returning him if the match wasn't perfect (P1_S07)
- Made all the majorette uniforms for the high school band while Keith watched proudly from the sidelines (P1_S07)
- Responded "interesting" and abruptly changed the subject when Keith called to announce he'd achieved the highest academic grade point average among 970 students in his first year of college (P1_S07)
- Threw away Keith's entire baseball card collection, including the whole St. Louis Cardinals team, while he was away at college—"In her mind, they were a worthless waste of time and space" (P1_S07)
- Stood beside her casket with Sandy in September 2001; they commented "that she looked peaceful for the first time that we could ever remember—the change in her face was miraculous" (P1_S07)
