## About Walter Hanson

Walter Hanson was the distinguished senior partner of Peat Marwick, whose judgment and leadership Keith came to respect deeply during his partnership years.

Hanson occupied the firm's highest leadership role and personally handled the rare and difficult task of forced partner resignations, approaching each instance "with guarded reluctance and trepidation" and personally explaining every case at the annual national meeting of partners (P1_S19). When Keith, newly elevated to partner in 1970, pressed for the removal of a negligent partner from the Cal-Maine Foods engagement, it was ultimately Hanson who accepted the recommendation and acted on it—a moment Keith described as "catharsis" (P1_S19).

Four years later, when Keith was managing the struggling Orlando office, he wrote Hanson a carefully composed letter recommending they close the office he had been sent to build. Keith was thirty-four and uncertain what the letter might mean for his career. Hanson's response revealed the quality of his leadership: he agreed with Keith's assessment, complimented him on his courage in making the recommendation, and personally assured him that "a meaningful career opportunity" would follow (P1_S20). Hanson kept that promise, and Keith's candor—risky as it felt—proved well-placed.

**Notable moments**

- Accepted Keith's recommendation to remove a negligent partner from the firm, despite the rarity of such action (P1_S19)
- Agreed with Keith's recommendation to close the Orlando office and personally assured him of future opportunity (P1_S20)
