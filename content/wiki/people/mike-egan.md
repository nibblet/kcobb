## About Mike Egan

Mike Egan was the founder and principal owner of Alamo Rent A Car and Keith's most important client during the Fort Lauderdale years, later becoming Keith's boss when Keith served as CEO of Alamo from 1995 to 1997.

Keith first met Mike around 1979 during a heated dispute over accounting matters. That initial confrontation resulted in a mutual respect that forged the basis for a long relationship spanning more than fifteen years (P1_S21). Mike insisted Keith assume personal responsibility for the Alamo account, which grew from approximately $30,000 in annual fees to $1.3 million over ten years as Alamo expanded from a small South Florida company to commanding more than 13 percent of the national market share. Keith describes Mike as "an exceptionally bright man and was extremely demanding, keeping me on my toes constantly," yet "always willing to pay top dollar for quality service" (P1_S21). Their relationship remained strictly professional—they never socialized casually but worked together intensely across the desk on strategic matters. Mike owned 94 percent of Alamo, representing essentially all his net worth, and though brilliant with market instincts, he "lacked the skills to manage a large-scale organization, a fact he grudgingly admitted" to Keith privately in 1995 (P1_S25). When Keith moved to Baltimore, Mike offered him a consulting retainer to remain available, which Keith accepted.

**Notable moments**

- Keith worked side-by-side with Mike negotiating his personal acquisition of controlling interest in Alamo from the Hanley Dawson family for $50 million in 1986, securing a critical $3 million unsecured loan when financing fell short (P1_S21)
- Recruited Keith as Alamo CEO in 1995 when the company was losing $50 million annually, offering to match Keith's KPMG compensation when Keith initially hesitated (P1_S21, P1_S25)
- Awarded Keith several gifts at a recognition dinner after the AutoNation sale, including an option to choose any car he wanted—Keith selected a BMW 840 CI (P1_S33)
