## About Jim Brocksmith

Jim Brocksmith was a managing partner in KPMG's Miami office and later deputy chairman and chief operating officer of the firm, whom Keith describes as "birds of a feather" and a close professional friend.

Keith developed a "wonderful and lasting friendship" with Jim during the mid-1980s when Jim was serving as managing partner of the Miami office and Keith was leading the Fort Lauderdale practice (P1_S21). The two were both young emerging leaders in the firm who bonded over similar intellects and value systems, working closely together in the South Florida marketplace. Their friendship continued after both left South Florida, and Keith "thoroughly enjoyed working directly with him" during his final years with KPMG (P1_S21).

When Jon Madonna and Jim Brocksmith were elected chairman and deputy chairman of KPMG in 1990, they quickly restructured the firm's management (P1_S22). The two leaders spearheaded a massive reorganization in 1993 that completely overhauled KPMG's structure around five industry segments rather than geography and functional silos—a "courageous and massive undertaking" (P1_S24). In one of the biggest surprises of Keith's career, Madonna and Brocksmith named him national managing partner for financial services, the firm's largest business unit (P1_S24).
