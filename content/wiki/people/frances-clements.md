## About Frances Clements

Frances Clements was Keith Cobb's mother, born in 1921 in the hill country of Lafayette and Pontotoc counties in north Mississippi, one of seven children in a relatively poor family.

After finishing high school at rural Toccopola, she worked as a beautician in Sarepta before marrying Bayne Cobb in 1940 and moving to Calhoun City, where she spent the next sixty-one years mostly on one parcel of property. Exceptionally bright and industrious, she launched a home-based business as an excellent seamstress, becoming the go-to seamstress for the city's most difficult sewing projects. She later worked outside the home at Lunceford's Bargain Store, Chandler's Drug Store, and Beasley Insurance Agency, considering each job a step up. Frances was a strict disciplinarian and taskmaster who created what Keith described as "a beehive of activity," ensuring her children were constantly engaged in school projects, music lessons, choirs, band practice, and endless chores. She was exacting in her standards and obsessed with Keith being perfect, though he often felt he could never measure up. Despite struggles with health issues, insecurity, and what Keith saw as emotional stress she inflicted on herself, she instilled in him his strong work ethic, fundamental value system, and self-discipline (P1_S07).
