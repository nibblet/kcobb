## About Fred Nabors

Fred Nabors was a local builder in Calhoun City and a longtime friend of Keith Cobb who acquired and meticulously restored the historic Mallory House.

In 1998, Nabors purchased the former home of Miss Frances Mallory—Keith's beloved high school English teacher—from the First Baptist Church. The 1905 house, which had been Miss Frances' only asset of significant value upon her death in 1994, was one of a handful of original residences in Calhoun City. Nabors undertook a painstaking restoration of the property to its original appearance (P1_S03). Keith describes Nabors as "a local developer" and characterizes his work on the property as meticulous (P1_S33). On what Keith calls "an impulse of nostalgia," he purchased the restored home from Nabors in 1998, vowing to keep it as a historical memory of Miss Frances. The house stands today in pristine condition with white picket fence, rose bushes, and manicured lawn—what Keith describes as qualifying "as a cover picture for Life magazine" (P1_S03).
