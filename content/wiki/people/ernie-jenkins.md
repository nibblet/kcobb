## About Ernie Jenkins

Ernie Jenkins was the president of Jackson Packing Company, a client Keith audited during his early years at Peat Marwick in Mississippi in the 1960s.

Jenkins is described as an "ultra-tough-guy president" who exhibited a hard-nosed management style (P1_S27). When Keith uncovered an elaborate embezzlement scheme by the company's livestock buyer, Ham Stockett—who had been manipulating cattle weights and numbers for years—Jenkins's response was blunt and immediate: he had Stockett brought in, witnessed his confession, and fired him on the spot. Rather than thanking Keith for the discovery, Jenkins simply asked why it had taken so long to find the fraud, providing Keith with an early lesson in the adage that "no good deed goes unpunished" (P1_S27).

Bill Norton once used the word "quixotic" incorrectly when describing Jenkins as someone "who changed his mind frequently," an error that sparked the beginning of Norton and Keith's lifelong vocabulary game (P1_S18). The misuse of the term—which actually means idealistic or impractical rather than changeable—became a memorable touchstone in Keith's relationship with his mentor.

**Notable moments**

- Demanded immediate confrontation when Keith discovered the livestock buyer embezzlement scheme, firing the employee on the spot after his confession (P1_S27)
- Gave Keith no credit for uncovering the fraud, only asking why it took so long to find it (P1_S27)
- Was characterized by Bill Norton as someone who changed his mind frequently, though Norton misused the word "quixotic" to describe this trait (P1_S18)
