## About Bayne Cobb

Bayne Cobb (1916–2009) was Keith Cobb's father, a twelfth-generation descendant of the Cobb family who settled in Banner, Mississippi in 1839. He spent thirty-five years working for The Justice Company as a truck driver, shipping clerk, and route salesman calling on grocers across Calhoun and Grenada counties, later managing a warehouse for State Cash Wholesale until retirement (P1_S06).

Born and raised in Banner, Bayne attended Bruce High School and Draughon's Business College in Memphis before returning home during the Great Depression. He married Frances Clements in 1940 and lived on South Madison Street in Calhoun City for the rest of his life. Keith describes learning "mostly through observation" from his father's quiet example rather than explicit instruction—values of conscientiousness, frugality, and steadiness. Bayne was "trustworthy and honest to the bone," never raised his voice in anger, and treated his children "like a partner" rather than subordinates (P1_S06). After Frances died in 2001, he remained active and independent, maintaining a vegetable garden until age ninety and driving until ninety-one, before passing away peacefully at a retirement home two days after his ninety-third birthday.

**Notable moments**

- When Keith wrecked the family car as a teenager, Bayne calmly helped right the vehicle and simply advised him to "be a little more careful on those dirt roads" (P1_S06)
- Paid off the family's $7,500 mortgage in seven years through double payments, never owned a car until saving $1,200 cash for a 1951 Plymouth (P1_S06)
- Became visibly emotional when Keith announced a summer internship offer of $425/month—more than Bayne had ever earned (P1_S06)

**A representative quote**
