## About Carter Dobbs

Dr. Carter Dobbs was Keith's scoutmaster in Troop 40 and the most significant teacher and role model of his formative years—a local dentist who brought the same rigor and serious expertise to scouting that he applied to his dental practice.

Dr. Dobbs devoted untold hours to Troop 40, wearing the uniform, making all the meetings, participating in hikes, and sleeping on the ground during campouts. He monitored each scout's development individually and held them to exacting standards: one had to do all the work to achieve the goals before basking in any rewards. He didn't believe in breaks, gimmies, or mulligans, yet his rigor never prevented the troop from having fun along the way (P1_S04). Under his direction, eleven young boys in Troop 40 attained the Eagle Scout award—an unusually large number for such a small community. Dr. Dobbs was a serious man who hardly ever laughed, but in the photograph of all eleven Eagle Scouts together, "a shadow of a smile sneaks out... a smile that says, 'Don't look at me; look at these guys'" (P1_S04). His positive leadership helped keep Keith grounded during these formative years and kept his feet on a positive pathway. Keith stayed in touch with him over the years, and when Keith received the Distinguished Eagle Scout Award in 2000, he made sure to mention Dr. Dobbs and pay tribute to the significant role he played in his life (P1_S04).

**Notable moments**

- Guided eleven scouts to Eagle Scout rank in Calhoun City, including Keith, an unusually large number for a small community (P1_S04)
- Keith attended his funeral in Calhoun City and "stood sadly beside his casket with tears welling up in my eyes" (P1_S04)
- Keith publicly honored him in acceptance remarks at the 2000 Distinguished Eagle Scout Award ceremony in Miami (P1_S04)
