## About Hank Watson

Hank Watson was a well-connected lawyer, banker, and political figure in Broward County whom Keith cold-called in the late 1970s as a newcomer seeking to establish himself in Fort Lauderdale's business community.

Watson was roughly twenty years older than Keith and had no prior connection to him when Keith reached out. Despite the boldness of the approach, Watson "jumped at the opportunity" to help. In their first meeting, he offered a practical bargain: if Keith would write a $500 personal check to sponsor the Easter Lily Ball his wife was chairing, Watson would "stand at the door and introduce you to every person who walks through it" (P1_S21). Both men kept their word, and that transaction launched a relationship that lasted for decades. Keith describes being unable to "begin to recite the ways these two men were helpful to me over the years," acknowledging "a dangling liability on my relationship balance sheet" for all Watson had done (P1_S21).

Years later, Watson was living at a retirement home in Pompano Beach, "still wheeling and dealing among the elderly residents there." Keith would see him occasionally at church, where Watson spoke to him "much the way a father might address a favorite son" (P1_S21).

**A representative quote**

> "I'll tell you what. My wife is chairing the Easter Lily Ball in a few weeks, and she's short on sponsorships. If you'll write me a $500 personal check, I'll stand at the door and introduce you to every person who walks through it!"  
> — _Hank Watson to Keith at their first meeting (P1_S21)_
