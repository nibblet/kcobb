## About Blue and Ruby Cole

Blue and Ruby Cole were Keith's next-door neighbors on South Madison Street in Calhoun City who served as "virtual surrogate parents" to Keith and his sister Sandy, giving them nicknames ("Bud" and "Dune") and showering them with affection their entire lives (P1_S08).

Blue's real name was Dewitt T. Cole. He and Ruby worked together at Harrelson Manufacturing Company during World War II, then Blue operated the local Standard Oil gasoline depot while Ruby did sewing at Ruth's Draperies. Though they had no children of their own, they "practically adopted" the Cobb children. Blue was a high-ranking Freemason (thirty-second or thirty-third degree) and sang in the Methodist choir; Ruby attended First Baptist and arranged flowers there every Sunday. Blue died in 1995 at age ninety; Ruby lived alone until her death in 2008 at age one hundred. When Ruby's will was read, she left her entire estate—surprisingly sizable for someone who had "lived practically as a pauper"—divided equally between Sandy and Keith's father, a reward for their devotion during her final years (P1_S08).

**Notable moments**

- Lied to Keith's mother about a head injury Keith accidentally caused her with a baseball bat, inventing a story to protect him from punishment while Blue "just winked at me and smiled" (P1_S08)
- Gave Keith a red Radio Flyer wagon over his mother's objections, dismissing her protests with "Well, he wanted it, and I wanted him to have it" (P1_S08)
- Drove Keith and Sandy to school in her old black Plymouth on cold mornings before the family owned a car (P1_S08)
- Recognized Keith's voice from sleep when nearly deaf and blind at age one hundred in the nursing home (P1_S08)

**A representative quote**

> "Oh, I'll bet you took the domino end table."
> — Ruby to Keith when he mentioned taking a piece of furniture, correctly identifying which one without hesitation (P1_S08)
