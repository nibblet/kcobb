## About Frances Hardin

Mrs. Frances Hardin taught bookkeeping and typing at Calhoun City High School, where Keith was her student in the late 1950s.

Her bookkeeping course had a lasting influence on Keith's career direction. The office and business orientation of her courses "more than likely had some influence on my decision to major in accounting in college," Keith recalled (P1_S02). Years later, when he enrolled in his first accounting courses at the University of Southern Mississippi, he found them "eerily reminiscent of Frances Hardin's bookkeeping course in high school" (P1_S13). Beyond the classroom, she supervised Keith's work as editor of the school newspaper, *The Loudspeaker*. She proofread his work and oversaw all his editorial activities in a small closet adjacent to her classroom—work Keith found challenging as he struggled to persuade students to write articles and meet deadlines. "Without her leadership, I probably would never have completed the first issue," he acknowledged (P1_S02). Keith counted her among the "outstanding educators who were so devoted to their students" at Calhoun City High School, to whom he was "deeply indebted" for their leadership (P1_S02).
