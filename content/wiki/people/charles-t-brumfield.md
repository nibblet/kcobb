## About Charles T. Brumfield

Charles T. Brumfield—known to family as Tommy, to many as Charles, but to Keith always as "Charlie"—was Keith's best friend for more than half a century, a friendship that began when both arrived as freshmen at Mississippi Southern College in autumn 1959.

Charlie came from the Biloxi-Gulfport area; Keith from Calhoun County in northern Mississippi. They were assigned to Forest Hall and eventually roomed together senior year in Bond Hall. Charlie was quieter and more studious than Keith, serving as "a guiding compass" during their college years. When Keith was elected SGA president, he immediately appointed Charlie as administrative assistant—Keith "always on the stage," Charlie "always in the wings, keeping my head straight." Charlie stood as best man at Keith and Dot's wedding. After earning a master's in economics at USM, he completed a doctorate at the University of South Carolina and spent decades teaching for the University of Maryland's overseas extension service, living in some twenty countries and visiting well over a hundred. He wrote an unpublished book on economic theory, *Basic Macroeconomic Equilibrium Analysis*. He married a German woman named Margot and raised their son Chuck largely on his own; years later, well past sixty, he married a young Thai woman. Keith admired Charlie as "the Indiana Jones that I, like many men, have only dreamed about," someone who "marched to his own drummer" with little ambition for wealth or fame (P1_S36).
