## About Bill Suttle

Bill Suttle was a senior SEC reviewing partner in Peat Marwick's Houston office who became Keith's crucial guide and advocate during the chaotic Cal-Maine Foods initial public offering in 1969.

Bill was "a serious professional, solemn and straight-laced" — "the quintessential technical accountant nearing the end of an illustrious career with the firm." He was "a man of few words—tough and rigid, yet practical and level headed," with maturity and judgment that proved critical when Keith, at age twenty-eight, found himself managing an extraordinarily complex SEC filing with virtually no partner support (P1_S18). Bill agreed to review Keith's work and sign off on behalf of the firm. Without him, Keith acknowledged, "I would never have made it through the engagement" (P1_S18).

After the engagement concluded, Bill became unexpectedly lavish in his praise — uncharacteristic for him. He provided written recommendations to regional partner-in-charge Gray Wakefield, stating that Cal-Maine was the most complex audit he had ever seen in his long career and that Keith's performance was the best he had ever witnessed in any professional setting. That recommendation "essentially became my ticket on a continuing bullet train" (P1_S18). Gray Wakefield trusted Bill's judgment implicitly and championed Keith's partnership candidacy based largely on Bill's advocacy, helping make Keith the youngest partner in Peat Marwick's seventy-three-year history at age twenty-nine (P1_S18).
