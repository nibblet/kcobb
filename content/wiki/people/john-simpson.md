## About John Simpson

John Simpson was Keith's good friend during his college years at the University of Southern Mississippi, a companion in both spontaneous adventures and calculated schemes.

The memoir depicts Simpson as an enthusiastic partner in Keith's memory demonstrations—playing "the role of ringmaster" in barrooms where he would convince patrons to bet money against Keith's ability to memorize fifty random words after hearing them once. Simpson would tell people Keith had a photographic memory, and the two friends would split their winnings, using the money to pay for "a lot of fun nights out on the town" (P1_S02). Beyond these profitable performances, Simpson also shared Keith's appetite for spontaneous travel. On a Saturday morning in February, Simpson suggested they go to Mardi Gras that weekend. Without hesitation, the two walked straight to the highway and hitchhiked to New Orleans with probably less than five dollars between them, arriving in the French Quarter that same afternoon to join the festivities (P1_S11).

**Notable moments**

- Played ringmaster in barroom bets, convincing patrons to wager against Keith's memory skills; they always won and split the money (P1_S02)
- Spontaneously proposed hitchhiking to Mardi Gras on a Saturday morning; they walked directly to the highway and made it to New Orleans the same day with less than five dollars between them (P1_S11)
