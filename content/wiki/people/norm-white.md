## About Norm White

Norm White was the regional partner in charge at Peat Marwick who oversaw Keith's transition to Orlando in 1971 and later his move to Fort Lauderdale in 1975.

White came across as what Keith described as "a crusty old curmudgeon or salty dog"—friendly and cordial, but blunt in his counsel (P1_S20). When Keith met with him in Atlanta to discuss opening the Orlando office, White offered little in the way of detailed guidance, admitting he had never opened an office himself and summing up his advice with: "Just go down there and beat the bushes. You'll do fine!" (P1_S20). Earlier, when discussing the Orlando opportunity over Jack Daniels and a cigar, White had challenged Keith's request to visit the city first, noting dryly that the executive committee had already decided Keith was "the best damn man in the firm to do this" (P1_S19). Despite his gruff manner, White proved supportive of Keith's career. When Keith wrote to Walter Hanson recommending the Orlando office be closed, White was the one who called a month later to offer Keith the managing partner role in Fort Lauderdale (P1_S20). Years later, when Keith was house-hunting before the Orlando move, he called White in Atlanta for advice about the purchase price; White laughed and told him, "Go for it. You can afford it" (P1_S33).
