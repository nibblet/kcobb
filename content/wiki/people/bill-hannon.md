## About Bill Hannon

Bill Hannon led KPMG's New York financial services group and served on Keith's national leadership team during the 1993 reorganization of the firm's Financial Services practice.

Hannon was what Keith called "the consummate major client professional." He led the New York group—one of ten regions Keith established when organizing the new Financial Services business unit from scratch. Hannon had a close relationship with Sandy Weill of Citicorp and was respected both in financial services circles and by the New York partners and staff. Keith had never worked with most of his leadership team members before the reorganization; his appointments were determined largely through due diligence on backgrounds and reputations of the most highly respected partners with financial services expertise, buttressed by consultations with people he respected. As it turned out, they were excellent choices for the most part (P1_S24).
