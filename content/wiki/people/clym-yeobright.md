## About Clym Yeobright

Clym Yeobright is the protagonist of Thomas Hardy's *Return of the Native*, a character Keith encountered in Miss Frances Mallory's senior English class and one who became a lasting metaphor for Keith's own relationship to his hometown.

Keith memorized and studied Hardy's novel under Miss Mallory's demanding instruction, slaving over the text along with Chaucer's prologue (P1_S02). More than fifty years later, the character remained vivid: Keith recalled "an apparently indelible mental image of Clym Yeobright, Thomas Hardy's native returning to Egdon Heath" whenever he drove back into Calhoun City (P1_S02). The image took on personal significance when Keith purchased Miss Mallory's historic home in 1998. He described the decision in explicit terms: "I think I sort of looked upon myself as a metaphorical Clym Yeobright (in Return of the Native) reconnecting with my roots" (P1_S03). The parallel was not merely literary sentiment—Keith's act of preserving the 1905 house where his teacher had lived for seventy-five years represented a literal return to care for something meaningful from his origins, much as Hardy's native son returned to Egdon Heath.
