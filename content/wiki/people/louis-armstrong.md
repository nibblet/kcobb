## About Louis Armstrong

Louis Armstrong was the performer of "What a Wonderful World," a recording that became deeply meaningful to Keith and Dot Cobb through their decades of world travel.

Keith and Dot heard Armstrong's famous recording "many times on Crystal Cruise ships, as they sailed out of every port" during their extensive travels to over seventy-five countries across all seven continents (P1_S29). The song's lyrics resonated with Keith's perspective on their life of exploration and discovery. After describing their wanderings through natural wonders, historical sites, and diverse cultures—from the pyramids of Egypt to the slums of Cairo, from ancient Angkor Wat to modern Singapore—Keith quoted the entire lyrics to "What a Wonderful World" near the end of his travel chapter (P1_S29). The song captured his reflective gratitude for the experiences he and Dot had shared. Keith also included "What a Wonderful World" in his broader catalog of favorite musical compositions, listing it among dozens of songs that "stir up my emotions" alongside works by Elvis Presley, The Beatles, and other artists who had touched him over time (P1_S31).

**A representative quote**

> "I see trees of green, red roses, too, / I see them bloom, for me and you. / And I think to myself, / What a wonderful world."
> 
> — Lyrics Keith quoted in full after reflecting on his and Dot's travels, concluding: "Yes, I indeed think to myself, what a wonderful world!" (P1_S29)
