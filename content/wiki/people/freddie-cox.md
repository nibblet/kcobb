## About Freddie Cox

Freddie Cox directed the glee club at Calhoun City High School and taught advanced piano lessons, serving as one of Keith's key music mentors during his formative years.

Cox was "a true virtuoso" at the piano and a memorable presence in Keith's musical education, despite what Keith describes as "many unusual personality quirks" (P1_S05). He taught in one of the small dressing rooms off the school auditorium, where his playing would fill the study hall with bursts of beauty amid the usual cacophony of student practice. Keith particularly remembered Cox's performances of "Clair de Lune," played "like no one I have heard since" — performances that left a lifelong impression on the young student (P1_S31). While Cox "didn't impart a lot of piano or music theory," Keith learned music appreciation from him and loved to hear him play, trying unsuccessfully to replicate his virtuosity (P1_S05). Keith sang in the glee club under Cox's direction throughout high school and counted him among the "outstanding educators who were so devoted to their students" at Calhoun City High School (P1_S02). Despite the acknowledged personality quirks, Keith considered Freddie "a great mentor" and remained "deeply indebted" to him for his leadership (P1_S02, P1_S05).

**Notable moments**

- Filled gaps in his lesson schedule by playing piano himself, performing "Clair de Lune" with such skill that it became one of Keith's lifelong favorite pieces (P1_S31)
- Directed the high school glee club in which Keith sang for four years (P1_S02)
