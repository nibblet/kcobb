## About Hy Indowsky

Hy Indowsky was one of two partners alongside Keith when Keith arrived as managing partner of the Fort Lauderdale Peat Marwick office in 1975, and an active figure in the Jewish community whose influence shaped the firm's early client base.

Indowsky, along with former managing partner Howard Miller, had built strong ties to the Jewish community in South Florida, which was reflected in the firm's clientele when Keith joined. The office's real estate and construction clients were "tightly tethered to the Jewish community, reflecting the influence of Howard Miller, the former managing partner, and Hy Indowsky, who were both active personalities in that segment of society" (P1_S21). Keith later reflected on Indowsky with an "enormous sense of pride" as part of the group who were "the cornerstones of my own success in Fort Lauderdale" (P1_S21).

Indowsky suffered a brain aneurysm, an experience that later gave Keith a point of connection when he encountered Senator Joe Biden on the train to Washington in the early 1990s. Keith noted that Biden "had recently suffered a brain aneurism, similar to my close friend Hy Indowsky, from the Fort Lauderdale Peat Marwick office, so we had at least a common starting point for a conversation" (P1_S30).
