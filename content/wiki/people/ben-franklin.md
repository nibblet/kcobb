## About Ben Franklin

Ben Franklin was a retail store in Calhoun City, Mississippi, located uptown about a mile from the Cobb home, where Keith's mother Frances sent him on frequent errands to purchase matching spools of thread for her seamstress business.

Beginning when Keith was around nine or ten years old, Frances enlisted him as her color coordinator, regularly sending him to the Ben Franklin store with fabric samples to buy matching thread (P1_S07). The walks there and back were about a mile each way. Keith quickly learned the importance of precision: if he did not secure a perfect match, he was immediately sent back to get it right. On one occasion, Frances sent him with a Noxzema container to try to match its dark blue color for a contrasting seam (P1_S07). The ladies at the store would often compliment him, saying things like, "Can you believe that child can get those colors right every single time?" (P1_S09). Over time, the Cobb household accumulated a large inventory of sewing thread with almost as many shades as the store's own inventory. Keith constructed racks with finishing nails to organize the spools by color (P1_S07).
