## About Roger Ballou

Roger Ballou was a senior executive from American Express whom Mike Egan had been considering for the CEO position at Alamo Rent A Car before choosing Keith; he joined instead as chief marketing officer and became Keith's closest business partner in the Alamo turnaround.

Keith agreed to bring Roger on board sight unseen, trusting Mike Egan's judgment—"uncharacteristic" for Keith, but "one of the best decisions I ever made" (P1_S25). The two executives "fit like two gloves" and "worked together like a symphony," developing what Keith describes as an uncanny ability to finish each other's sentences. Though audiences often assumed Keith handled finance and Roger handled marketing, Keith notes the opposite was true: "Roger was a far better financial analyst than I was," while Keith's strengths lay in management and leadership of large organizations (P1_S25). They met daily at 7 a.m. to recalibrate tactics, then executed strategy in complementary directions. Together they developed a seventy-five-slide deck covering Alamo's critical metrics and made joint presentations, alternating commentary at one-to-two-minute intervals without notes—"an unusual display of executive leadership" to observers (P1_S25). Keith calls Roger "one of the smartest people I have ever met" and credits their partnership with transforming Alamo from a $50 million loss to $40 million profit and a $625 million sale in under two years (P1_S25).

**Notable moments**

- Met with Keith daily at 7 a.m. before other staff arrived to recalibrate tactics (P1_S25)
- Challenged internal executives who wanted to lower rates when Hertz raised theirs, arguing "you lost $50 million last year with a 13 percent market share. Why would you want more market share at lower rates?" (P1_S25)
- Joined Keith on the board of Alliance Data Systems Corporation, where they continued their symbiotic relationship and still finish each other's sentences (P1_S25, P1_S26)
