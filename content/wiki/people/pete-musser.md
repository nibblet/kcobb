## About Pete Musser

Pete Musser was chairman and CEO of Safeguard Scientific and a major client of KPMG in Philadelphia who became a member of Keith's "Hall of Fame" for his generosity in opening doors and initiating relationships.

Keith first met Pete at a luncheon meeting shortly after arriving in Philadelphia in 1991, an encounter memorable for Pete bringing his Irish setter to lunch and seating the dog next to his place at the table (P1_S23). As Keith was leaving that lunch, Pete matter-of-factly asked for a $10,000 check for the United Way, insisting that "a guy like you can't be the managing partner of KPMG and not be a member of the Alexis de Tocqueville Society" (P1_S23). Keith sent the check that afternoon, recalling Larry Horner's early advice about civic engagement. Pete called a few days later to say he admired Keith's reaction to his admitted pushiness, then arranged for Keith's appointment to the board of directors of the Alexis de Tocqueville Society—a move that launched a series of new relationships in the city. Pete later arranged Keith's appointment to the Philadelphia Boy Scout Council as well (P1_S23). Years later, Pete provided another nudge that led Keith and Dot to increase their United Way pledge to $10,000 annually, a level they maintained for over twenty years (P1_S38). For Keith, Pete exemplified a valuable business fundamental: "There are all sorts of quid pro quos in life, and they can contribute to success if one plays his cards smartly" (P1_S23).
