## About Elvis Presley

Keith met Elvis Presley briefly in 1956 at the Mid-South Fair Talent Competition in Memphis, when Keith was fifteen and Elvis was twenty-one, at a breakout point in his career having just signed with RCA.

The encounter took place in an alleyway behind the stage, where Elvis refused to make an appearance despite Colonel Parker's efforts to showcase him. Keith and his four Dixieland jazz bandmates watched Elvis throw a wristwatch back and forth with a friend while he chatted with them. Elvis bragged about winning a teddy bear at a carnival booth and ridiculed Colonel Parker for being "an unbridled promoter" (P1_S30). Keith was struck by Elvis's obstinacy and thought him foolish to miss such a publicity opportunity. Years later, reflecting on this minor incident, Keith recognized it as a lesson "that minor blips do not sidetrack the momentum of a career trajectory" (P1_S30). The encounter remained memorable to Keith throughout his life, though he acknowledged it was surely memorable only to him, not to Elvis—one of many celebrity meetings that "left indelible impressions on me, life lessons that have lighted my pathways for years" (P1_S30).

**Notable moments**

- Met Elvis backstage at the Mid-South Fair Talent Competition in Memphis, circa 1956, when Keith's Dixieland jazz band competed for a spot on the Ted Mack Amateur Hour (P1_S05, P1_S30)
- Observed Elvis refusing to go on stage despite Colonel Parker's promotion efforts, throwing a wristwatch in the alley behind the stage (P1_S30)
