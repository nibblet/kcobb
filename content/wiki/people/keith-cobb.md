## About Keith Cobb

Keith Cobb is the author of this family memoir, a towheaded country boy from the red clay hills of Calhoun County, Mississippi, who rose to national leadership in professional accounting and business.

Born in 1941 in Calhoun City, Mississippi, Keith was a thirteenth-generation Cobb in America, descended from Joseph Cobb, Jr., who arrived in Jamestown shortly after 1607. His family had lived in Calhoun County for roughly a hundred years before his birth (P1_S01). He grew up in a small town of fewer than two thousand people, a skinny, curious kid with a serious cowlick who was active in school band, academics, and various jobs. He married Dorothy (Dot) Hill on June 15, 1963, after a courtship that began in summer 1961 when they discovered shared values of honesty, integrity, and work ethic (P1_S12). Keith spent thirty-two years at KPMG, rising to national managing partner of Financial Services—the firm's largest business unit, comprising 40 percent of revenues and over four thousand professionals. In 1995, he left KPMG to become vice chairman and CEO of Alamo Rent A Car (P1_S24). He and Dot raised three sons—Paul, John, and Mark—and became grandparents to five grandchildren: Miranda, Jordan, Avery, Allison, and Ethan Keith (P1_S35).

**Notable moments**

- Sang "By the Light of the Silvery Moon" with Dot Hill at a high school prom when they were fourteen or fifteen, their earliest recorded performance together (P1_S12)
- Delivered a flawless forty-minute strategic presentation without notes to four hundred KPMG partners in Orlando, silencing critics and transforming the Financial Services practice (P1_S24)
- Celebrated his fiftieth wedding anniversary with the entire family on a Caribbean cruise (P1_S35)
