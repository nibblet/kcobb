## About Pete Fountain

Pete Fountain was a professional jazz clarinetist whom Keith encountered fortuitously during college in Tallahassee, Florida.

Keith met Fountain at the Lafayette Motel after a concert at Florida State University. When Keith mentioned he played the clarinet, Fountain loaned him one of his clarinets and they sat out by the motel swimming pool at about midnight with a couple of his band members playing Dixieland jazz (P1_S02). This brief, spontaneous jam session stands out as Keith's final meaningful engagement with the clarinet—an instrument he had played proficiently throughout high school in the band, Dixieland groups, and dance bands, but never touched again after graduation except for this one night with Fountain.

**Notable moments**

- Loaned Keith one of his clarinets for an impromptu midnight Dixieland jazz session by the motel pool in Tallahassee, with a couple of his band members joining (P1_S02)

**A representative quote**

> "Every time I hear the song, 'Just a Closer Walk,' memories of that night spring to my mind."
> — _Keith recalling the midnight session with Pete Fountain_ (P1_S02)
