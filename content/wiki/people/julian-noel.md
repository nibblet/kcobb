## About Julian Noel

Julian Noel was a career manager at Peat Marwick in Jackson, Mississippi, who had worked in the office for more than thirty years by the time Keith joined the firm in 1963.

Keith describes Julian as "the positive Julian Noel," contrasting him with other colleagues who were taciturn or goofy (P1_S18). Though Keith notes he "didn't necessarily follow or buddy up with any of them," he learned from Julian and the other professionals in the Jackson office during his early years at the firm. Julian handled some of the old-line clients and played a crucial role in one of Keith's most memorable early fraud cases. While auditing Alvis & Company, a small municipal bond dealer, Julian came to the office to help Keith learn the complicated bond trading business. His instincts proved invaluable: Julian picked up a stack of cancelled checks and said, "Hmmm, these checks feel funny" (P1_S27). Upon close scrutiny, they discovered the checks were slightly shorter than normal—about a quarter of an inch—revealing an intricate embezzlement scheme where the bookkeeper had been clipping off her endorsements and forging customer names. Keith credits Julian's "uncanny instincts and years of experience" for catching the fraud, stating plainly, "Had it not been for Julian's uncanny instincts and years of experience, I would never have noticed this embezzlement" (P1_S27).
