## About Ronald Reagan

Ronald Reagan was the 40th President of the United States and a client of Myles Martel, the premier communication consultant who coached Keith Cobb before his pivotal 1993 Orlando presentation to four hundred KPMG financial services partners.

Myles Martel had been Ronald Reagan's debate coach during his candidacy for president (P1_S24). When Keith was preparing for the career-defining Orlando meeting that would establish his leadership of KPMG's newly reorganized Financial Services business unit, KPMG's public relations firm recommended Martel as a communication consultant. Keith spent hours in Martel's studio near his Philadelphia home, standing behind a "presidential-like podium" and practicing delivery with Martel's stylistic guidance (P1_S24). The connection to Reagan's debate preparation underscored Martel's credentials as someone who worked with high-stakes communicators.

Years later, Keith encountered another Reagan connection through author Tom Clancy, who described how his first novel, *The Hunt for Red October*, reached a mass audience. Clancy had given a copy to an Argentine woman seated next to him on an airplane, who passed it to her friend President Ronald Reagan. When Reagan was interviewed on television and asked what he was reading for fun, he held up the book and said, "Well, I'm reading this excellent book called *The Hunt for Red October*" (P1_S30). After that endorsement, Clancy recalled, "no further marketing was necessary."

**Notable moments**

- Myles Martel, Reagan's former debate coach, helped Keith prepare for the Orlando presentation using techniques refined with presidential clients (P1_S24)
- President Reagan's televised endorsement of Tom Clancy's novel illustrated to Keith "how serendipity, coupled with a little skill, can dramatically change one's life" (P1_S30)
