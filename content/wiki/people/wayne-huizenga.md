## About Wayne Huizenga

Wayne Huizenga was a high-profile serial entrepreneur in South Florida and founder of AutoNation who acquired Alamo Rent A Car from Mike Egan in 1996–97.

Wayne was a competitor during Keith's Fort Lauderdale years; Arthur Andersen handled all of Wayne's businesses, including Blockbuster Video, while Keith's firm KPMG served Mike Egan and Alamo (P1_S21). Keith first approached Wayne in a parking lot at the Performing Arts Center to suggest that AutoNation acquire Alamo, citing synergies in the volume of cars bought and sold by both organizations. Wayne initially brushed him off, saying he didn't know the car rental business and wasn't interested—but his right-hand man Steve Berrard called the next day to discuss it further (P1_S25). After Mike Egan rejected a Cendant offer, negotiations with Wayne moved quickly. Using what Keith called his "legendary intuitive judgment," Wayne rounded the price up to $625 million with only modest due diligence, and the deal closed in early 1997 (P1_S25). At closing, Wayne offered Keith a new contract with incentive conditions, but Keith declined, preferring to collect on his existing severance arrangement. Wayne seemed surprised, remarking, "So, you're just going to take your money and run?" Keith responded affirmatively, and though they remained friendly, Keith suspected Wayne never forgot that he didn't jump at the chance to work for him (P1_S25).

**Notable moments**

- Initially dismissed Keith's suggestion that AutoNation acquire Alamo, saying he knew nothing about car rental—then had Steve Berrard follow up the next day (P1_S25)
- Closed the Alamo acquisition for $625 million using intuitive judgment rather than extensive due diligence (P1_S25)
- Later doubled down by acquiring National Car Rental, but AutoNation ultimately lost approximately $1.2 billion on the combined car rental operations over four years (P1_S25)

**A representative quote**

> "Come on. Give me a break. You buy cars, you rent them for a while and then you sell them. How complicated can that be?"
> — Wayne to Keith on the night of closing, when Keith was explaining the complexities of the car rental business; Wayne "found out the hard way" (P1_S25)
