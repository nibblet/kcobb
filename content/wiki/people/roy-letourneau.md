## About Roy Letourneau

Roy Letourneau was one of Keith's neighbors in Altamonte Springs, Florida, and became a fast friend when the Cobb family moved to Orlando in August 1971.

Roy and his family lived next door to the Cobbs during Keith's pioneering years opening Peat Marwick's Orlando office. The Letourneaus were "extremely helpful in getting us acclimated to our new surroundings" and remained valued friends for over four decades (P1_S20). In the early 1980s, Roy owned a motor home rental business in Texas. Keith describes their enduring friendship with plain gratitude: "In late 2014, I had the opportunity to spend a wonderful day with an ailing Roy Letourneau in Dallas, just a month before he died" (P1_S20).

**Notable moments**

- Became fast friends with the Cobb family when they arrived in Orlando as "true pioneers" in August 1971, helping them adjust to their new surroundings in Altamonte Springs (P1_S20)

- Rented the Cobb family a twenty-six-foot motor home from his Texas business for their memorable three-week Western vacation touring Colorado, Arizona, and New Mexico around 1982 (P1_S39)

- Keith spent a full day with Roy in Dallas in late 2014, just a month before Roy's death, closing out more than forty years of valued friendship (P1_S20)
