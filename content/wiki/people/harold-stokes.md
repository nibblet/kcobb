## About Harold Stokes

Harold Stokes was the regional partner at KPMG who orchestrated Keith's transfer from Fort Lauderdale to Baltimore in 1989 and later to Philadelphia in 1991.

Harold first appeared in early 1989 when he visited Fort Lauderdale and casually mentioned issues in the Baltimore office—comments Keith recognized as intentional, not idle gossip. Two weeks later, Harold returned with a formal proposition that Keith transfer to Baltimore as managing partner. When Keith expressed hesitation, Harold's response was decisive (P1_S21). A year later, during a management committee meeting in Atlanta, Harold delivered another career-defining moment with characteristic dry humor when the committee decided Keith should move to Philadelphia. Asked what to say to the "crazy idea," Harold quipped: "My friend, I think you may as well lie down on the track, because the train has already run over you!" (P1_S22). The remark captured both Harold's influence in Keith's career trajectory and his ability to frame difficult decisions with levity. Harold served as a trusted advisor during Keith's transitions between major offices, reading the firm's needs and matching them to Keith's capabilities.
