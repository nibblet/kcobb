## About Bill Norton

Bill Norton was the managing partner of the Peat Marwick Jackson office and Keith Cobb's most important mentor during his early career at the firm—a relationship Keith described as "one of the finest friends I have ever had."

Norton led the Jackson office when Keith joined Peat Marwick in July 1963, having previously managed the independent firm Dick D. Quinn & Co. before its 1961 merger with Peat Marwick. From Keith's first day, Norton "wrapped his arms around me from the get-go and never let go until the day he died," promoting Keith rapidly and giving him enormous responsibility (P1_S18). Norton had "an unflagging confidence" in Keith's ability and "promoted me everywhere he went." His support proved decisive: Norton made the forceful nomination that led to Keith becoming the youngest partner in Peat Marwick's seventy-three-year history at age twenty-nine (P1_S18).

Norton was "an odd duck" with the demeanor of "the stereotypical absent-minded professor," sometimes tending his orchids during business crises. Yet he was "extremely intelligent and financially astute" (P1_S18). He was also a vocabulary enthusiast who engaged Keith in an unspoken word-riddle game that lasted over forty years, using "arcane words and phrases that induced a trip to the dictionary" and owning a huge dictionary Keith eventually emulated (P1_S18, P1_S32). Norton counseled Keith to "do what was right, even in the face of adversity," telling him: "sometimes adversities set in, and you just have to face into the wind" (P1_S21).

**Notable moments**

- First met Keith during the Peat Marwick campus interview at USM alongside Harold Dey, then stayed in touch and invited Keith to visit the Jackson office in mid-December 1962 (P1_S16)
- Showed up unexpectedly at Keith's home on a Sunday afternoon in June 1970 to tell him he'd been elected partner (P1_S18)
- Demonstrated absolute confidence by signing the L&A Contracting audit report after asking only "Cobb, is it OK for me to sign this report?"—a pivotal moment when Keith realized "the burden of decision-making" (P1_S18)
- Persuaded by Keith to recommend firing the negligent Cal-Maine partner to the executive committee, despite twenty years of partnership (P1_S19)

**A representative quote**

> "Keith, Mr. Norton would like for you to call him Bill!"
> — Gloria Smith, Norton's secretary, after Keith was promoted to manager (P1_S18)
