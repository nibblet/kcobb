## About Ruby Cole

Ruby Cole was Keith Cobb's next-door neighbor on South Madison Street in Calhoun City, Mississippi, who served as a virtual surrogate parent to Keith and his sister Sandy throughout their childhood.

Ruby and her husband Blue worked hard their entire lives, well into their seventies. She labored alongside Blue on the factory floor at Harrelson Manufacturing Company during World War II, and later did sewing and odd jobs at Ruth's Draperies (P1_S08). A devout member of First Baptist Church, she was responsible for the flower arrangements every Sunday for as far back as Keith could remember. Ruby and Blue never had children but gave Keith and Sandy nicknames—calling Keith "Bud" and Sandy "Dune"—and were extremely generous with their time, playing with them and teaching them things (P1_S08). She was openly critical of the strict discipline the Cobb children lived under and didn't hesitate to choose sides when they were being punished. During Keith's teenage years, Ruby moved to another part of town, though Keith made a point of visiting whenever he was back in Calhoun City. She died peacefully in 2008 at age one hundred, leaving her entire estate—surprisingly sizable for someone who had lived "practically as a pauper"—to be divided equally between Sandy and Keith's father, Bayne (P1_S08).

**Notable moments**

- Lied to Keith's mother about a head injury Keith accidentally caused with a baseball bat, claiming she had stumbled on stairs, to protect Keith from punishment (P1_S08)
- Drove Keith and Sandy to school in her old black Plymouth whenever weather was particularly cold or inclement, before the Cobb family owned a car (P1_S08)
- Recognized Keith's voice from a dead sleep when he visited her in the nursing home when she was nearly one hundred, almost deaf and practically blind (P1_S08)
- Correctly guessed from her nursing home bed that Keith had taken "the domino end table" from her house—the table where she and Blue had played dominos with Keith as a child (P1_S08)

**A representative quote**

> "Well, he wanted it, and I wanted him to have it. That's just all there is to it, Frances. You should just cool down!"
> — Ruby's response to Keith's mother after bringing Keith a Radio Flyer wagon (P1_S08)
