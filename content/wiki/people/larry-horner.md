## About Larry Horner

Larry Horner was the managing partner of KPMG's Miami office in the mid-1970s and later became chairman of the firm. He served as an important mentor to Keith during the early Fort Lauderdale years, offering strategic counsel on building a network as an outsider in a tight-knit community.

When Keith arrived in Fort Lauderdale in 1975, he invited Larry to lunch seeking advice on how to establish himself. Larry had been "incredibly successful networking as a newcomer and an 'outsider' in the tight-knit Miami community" and shared advice that "became a fundamental part of my relationship strategies for the balance of my career." Most notably, he counseled Keith to use personal funds to invest in his own success rather than waiting for the firm to act, explaining how he had made a personal contribution to the University of Miami and was immediately appointed to its board of directors (P1_S21). As KPMG chairman in 1987, Larry organized regional partner meetings to explain and garner support for the firm's merger with KMG Main Hurdman—a merger Keith opposed and famously quipped would mean "Kiss Peat Marwick Goodbye" (P1_S21). Decades later, Keith visited Larry, who was in poor health and confined to a wheelchair, and thanked him for his sage advice from forty years earlier. Larry "smiled and looked pleased that I remembered" (P1_S21).

**Notable moments**

- Advised Keith over lunch to invest personal funds in community involvement, citing his own University of Miami board appointment as an example (P1_S21)
- As KPMG chairman, led the 1987 merger with KMG Main Hurdman that ultimately resulted in dropping "Peat Marwick" from the firm name (P1_S21)
