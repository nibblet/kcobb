## About Libba Jane Harrelson

Libba Jane Harrelson was the church organist in Calhoun City and one of Keith's music teachers during his formative years.

She taught beginning-level piano lessons in one of the small dressing rooms on either side of the stage in the school auditorium, where students practiced during study hall periods (P1_S31). Keith describes her as "a dedicated professional who gave us unusual attention and instruction" (P1_S05). Her influence extended beyond formal lessons—when Keith was sick one summer, she visited his home and brought him a gift of Van Cliburn's stereo recording from the famous Rachmaninoff piano competition in Moscow. Keith cherished that album and played it hundreds of times (P1_S05). The concerto became one of Keith's lifelong favorites, though Libba Jane likely had no idea how much he would enjoy it for the rest of his life (P1_S31). Keith did have one grievance with her: she kept the church organ locked, with the key on a ribbon around her neck, never allowing him to touch it despite his desperate desire to play and explore how it worked (P1_S05).
