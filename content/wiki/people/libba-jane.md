## About Libba Jane

Libba Jane Harrelson was the church organist in Calhoun City and one of Keith's piano teachers during his childhood, offering dedicated professional instruction in music.

Libba Jane taught beginning-level piano students in one of the small dressing rooms off the auditorium stage where study hall was held (P1_S31). She was known as a dedicated professional who gave her students unusual attention and instruction (P1_S05). When Keith was sick one summer, she visited him at home and brought a gift that would prove formative: the Van Cliburn stereo recording from the famous Rachmaninoff piano competition in Moscow. Keith cherished that album and played it hundreds of times, though Libba Jane surely had no idea how much he would enjoy it for the rest of his life (P1_S05, P1_S31). The recording introduced Keith to Tchaikovsky's Piano Concerto No. 1 in B Flat Minor, which became one of his favorite musical compositions (P1_S31).

Keith had one memorable beef with Libba Jane: she kept the church organ locked, with the key on a ribbon around her neck. He desperately wanted to play the organ and see how it worked, but she was resolute in not letting him touch it (P1_S05).
