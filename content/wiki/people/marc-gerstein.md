## About Marc Gerstein

Marc Gerstein was a visiting scholar at MIT and recognized thought leader in organizational design who served as Keith's strategic consultant during the 1993 reorganization of KPMG's Financial Services practice.

Keith found Gerstein through referrals from KPMG's executive offices in New York when preparing for the pivotal Orlando conference of four hundred financial services partners. Gerstein was "a brilliant man, nothing short of a genius," and Keith "benefitted enormously from his consultation," though working with him "wasn't easy. In fact, it was miserable. But it was fruitful" (P1_S24). Gerstein challenged Keith to think beyond immediate tactical issues and pushed him to set aside preconceptions about the public accounting business. Together they developed a cohesive five-point business strategy centered on superior client service, risk management, targeting market leaders, knowledge management, and talent allocation. The experience proved transformative for Keith personally: Gerstein convinced him that his lifelong multi-tasking and pivoting skills, while valuable, were "insufficient to accomplish the transformation" he faced (P1_S24). Years later, when Keith joined Alamo Rent A Car, he drew directly on these lessons, calling again on "my KPMG financial service experiences and the earlier advice of Marc Gerstein to develop meaningful strategies around five key sectors of the business" (P1_S25).

**Notable moments**

- Forced Keith to "wrap my head around something beyond the immediate issues" and strip away provincial thinking during the 1993 KPMG reorganization (P1_S24)
- Co-developed the five-point strategic plan that Keith presented at the career-defining Orlando conference (P1_S24)
