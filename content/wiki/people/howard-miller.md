## About Howard Miller

Howard Miller was the managing partner of the Peat Marwick Fort Lauderdale office before Keith Cobb, and an active figure in the Jewish business community of South Florida.

Miller's leadership shaped the Fort Lauderdale office's initial identity and client base. Under his tenure, the practice was dominated by real estate and construction clients and was "tightly tethered to the Jewish community," reflecting both Miller's and partner Hy Indowsky's influence in that segment of society (P1_S21). When Keith arrived in Fort Lauderdale in 1975, he inherited an office of two other partners and twenty-four professionals, operating from offices on Sunrise Boulevard outside the city's main commercial center. Keith noted that he needed to establish his own identity in the community, partly because the office bore Miller's distinctive stamp (P1_S21).

**Notable moments**

- Served as managing partner of the Fort Lauderdale office before transferring to Switzerland in 1975, at which point Keith Cobb was selected to replace him (P1_S20)

- Built a practice closely connected to the Jewish business community and shaped the office's early client portfolio around real estate and construction (P1_S21)
