## About David Rush

David Rush was the owner and CEO of ACR Electronics in Fort Lauderdale and one of the two best-connected businessmen Keith identified when he arrived in the city in 1975—a relationship that began with a cold call and grew into a cherished friendship lasting decades.

David was approximately twenty years older than Keith, and the two had no prior connection points when Keith picked up the phone to introduce himself and ask for help getting established in the business community (P1_S21). David "jumped at the opportunity and went out of [his] way to assist," though Keith notes he was never quite sure why. Over the years, David helped Keith in ways Keith "cannot begin to recite," contributing to what Keith describes as "a dangling liability on my relationship balance sheet" because he could never fully reciprocate (P1_S21).

David Rush passed away a few years before Keith wrote the memoir. Keith sat at his funeral at Temple Beth Torah in Fort Lauderdale with "big tears streaming down [his] face," reflecting on a relationship that had begun simply with boldness and a telephone call to a stranger (P1_S21).
