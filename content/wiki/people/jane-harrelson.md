## About Jane Harrelson

Jane Harrelson (referred to as "Libba Jane Harrelson" in the memoir) was the church organist in Calhoun City and a piano teacher who taught beginning-level students in one of the small dressing rooms adjacent to the school auditorium during Keith's seventh and eighth grade years.

Keith describes her as "a dedicated professional who gave us unusual attention and instruction" (P1_S05). She demonstrated a keen awareness of musical culture beyond small-town Mississippi: when Van Cliburn won the first Tchaikovsky International Piano Competition in Moscow in 1958, she recognized the historical significance of the event and presented Keith with a gift of the soundtrack recording—Tchaikovsky's Piano Concerto No. 1 in B Flat Minor performed by Cliburn (P1_S05, P1_S31). Keith cherished that album and played it hundreds of times; he still has it stored among his relics and considers the concerto "one of the most powerful pieces of classical music ever composed" (P1_S31). She also made a personal visit to Keith's home when he was sick one summer to bring him this gift (P1_S05). Keith did have one complaint: she kept the church organ locked with the key on a ribbon around her neck, refusing to let him play it despite his eagerness to explore how it worked (P1_S05).

**Notable moments**

- Visited Keith at home when he was sick and brought him the Van Cliburn recording of Tchaikovsky's Piano Concerto No. 1, which became one of his most treasured musical gifts (P1_S05, P1_S31)
- Taught beginning piano lessons in the dressing room next to the school auditorium, where her students' practice sessions formed part of the "competing cacophony" Keith heard during study hall (P1_S31)
