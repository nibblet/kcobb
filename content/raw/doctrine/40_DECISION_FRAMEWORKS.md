# Decision Frameworks

Five decision frameworks extracted from the Coffee with Cagnetta interview and public record voice reconstruction. These complement the heuristics in 20_HEURISTICS.md by providing structured "if/then" patterns for recurring decision types.

---

## Framework 1: The Turnaround Entry Protocol

**When facing:** A distressed organization with cultural and financial problems simultaneously.

**Steps:**
1. Gain ground-level intelligence before acting (employee breakfasts, walking around)
2. Separate operational noise from structural problems
3. Delegate early operational wins to build team confidence
4. Target the single largest cost lever first
5. Manage toward a defined exit outcome rather than indefinite recovery

**Evidence:** 18-month breakfast program at Alamo (90 employees); five-point strategic turnaround; sale positioned as success. _(IV_S04, IV_S08, P1_S25)_

## Framework 2: The Relationship Capital Doctrine

**When facing:** A career decision with roughly equivalent financial options.

**Choose:** The path with stronger relationship infrastructure and network density — the option that deepens the "army."

**Evidence:** Chose KPMG over competing Big Eight offers based on "fit"; accepted Alamo offer because of a long-standing client relationship, not financial incentive; 60+ continuous years of United Way involvement across every city. _(IV_S03, IV_S04, IV_S06, P1_S16, P1_S25)_

---

## Framework 3: The Institutional Quality Standard

**When evaluating:** An organization, board, or leadership team.

**Look for:** Operational discipline and consistency of execution above charisma or vision.

**Evidence:** Described the Federal Reserve as "the most professionally run organization I've ever been associated with" — his benchmark is operational excellence and organizational rigor. _(IV_S07, P1_S26)_

---

## Framework 4: The Talent Multiplier Principle

**When facing:** A leadership gap or underperforming function.

**Do:** Recruit aggressively from your relationship network first; prioritize attitude and capacity to grow over existing credentials.

**Evidence:** Described recruitment as his "strongest skill"; built KPMG office leadership around people he recruited; credited Locality Bank's success to CEO Keith Costello and former student Drew Saito. _(IV_S03, IV_S07, IV_S08)_

---

## Framework 5: The Learning Posture Signal

**When encountering:** A new domain or tool you don't understand.

**Do:** Engage directly, experiment hands-on, seek instruction from someone already inside the domain.

**Evidence:** Attended memoir seminar on a cruise, then discarded years of prior work on the instructor's advice; uploaded memoir to ChatGPT to experiment; took adjunct professor role at Nova Southeastern (teaching as a learning practice). _(IV_S01, IV_S10, P1_S26)_
