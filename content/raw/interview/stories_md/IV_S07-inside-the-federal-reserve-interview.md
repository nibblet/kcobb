# Inside the Federal Reserve (Interview)

*Transcript edited lightly for clarity and readability; wording and voice preserved.*

## Full Text

**Cagnetta:** But you've been doing some business things too—you've been on the Federal Reserve Board.

**Keith:** I was. I served on the Federal Reserve Board for six years.

**Cagnetta:** What's that like?

**Keith:** I tell people the Federal Reserve is, in my judgment, the most professional organization I've ever been associated with in my whole career—and that's saying a lot. I've seen a lot of companies and organizations in my lifetime, but I would put the Federal Reserve at the very top in how it's managed and professionally run—notwithstanding whatever economic policies they may have.

**Cagnetta:** Sure.

**Keith:** From an operational point of view, it's a very professional organization. I served on the board for six years and thoroughly enjoyed it. It was educational. I had to go back and get one of my old college money-and-banking economics books to bone up.

**Cagnetta:** Bone up on micro or macro—which one?

**Keith:** Either one. Both of them.

**Cagnetta:** Right.

**Keith:** Yeah, it was an interesting exercise. I've served on a number of corporate boards across the country.

**Cagnetta:** Yeah. Currently Locality still?

**Keith:** I'm on the board of Locality Bank—I was here with one of the organizers when we started that bank about three and a half years ago. It's been successful and it's going very well, and I'm still hanging in there. I was an original investor and part of organizing it.

**Cagnetta:** Well, you've recruited some good people there, right?

**Keith:** There are very good people there—that's been the key to their success. They've got a wonderful CEO in Keith Costello.

**Cagnetta:** Yes, he does. He's an excellent leader and an excellent banker.

**Keith:** Drew Saito—you probably know Drew—Drew was a student of mine at Nova Southeastern when I was adjunct teaching in the business school out at Nova. That's where I first met him.

**Cagnetta:** Yeah, sure.

**Keith:** He was a student.

**Cagnetta:** Wow.

**Keith:** Actually I didn't recruit him—he recruited me. When they were organizing the bank he came to me and said I needed to be involved. That's how I got connected.
