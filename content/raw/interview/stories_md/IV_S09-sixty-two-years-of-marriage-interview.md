## Full Text

**Keith:** I didn't know that—I should have said—we grew up in the same small town in Mississippi. We actually lived on the same street, a block apart.

**Cagnetta:** Wow.

**Keith:** So we've known each other since we were little kids. But we never dated until we were in college—we went to different colleges. It was kind of an odd series of circumstances. That's our background. We knew what we were getting into, I guess.

**Cagnetta:** Yeah. For a while you had a house in your old hometown?

**Keith:** We had a house there for about 25 years. We bought it from my old school teacher—my high school English teacher. She died and willed it to the church; a friend of mine, a developer, came along and restored it to its original state. It's the oldest house in the community there. We just sold it this past year. All of our friends and relatives were dying off or moving away, so we had less and less reason to keep going back. We kept it for 25 years. Beautiful place.

**Cagnetta:** Yeah, it's great. You've done a lot of things. What are some of your fun things to do here in South Florida?

**Keith:** Well, one of the fun things—I talked about relationships—we enjoy, my wife and I, getting out and mixing and mingling with friends we've developed over the years. We were just commenting this past weekend, on our anniversary—we think we're probably busier than most people.

**Cagnetta:** Yeah. No—I always see you out. And you've moved recently, within the last year, out to John Knox Village—

**Keith:** —which is a wonderful retirement community. There's more to do there than one can possibly do, so you have to pick and choose. We're spending a lot of time the last several months getting acquainted with all that's available there. We travel a lot—we have traveled a lot over the years—and we're probably doing less of that now than we used to because we're getting older, right? That happens.

**Cagnetta:** Yeah, that happens.
