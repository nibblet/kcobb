# Sixty Years of United Way (Interview)

_Transcript edited lightly for clarity and readability; wording and voice preserved._

## Full Text

**Cagnetta:** All right—you're back down here, but you've been involved in nonprofit for a long time.

**Keith:** I have been. Yep, I have been.

**Cagnetta:** What was your first foray into nonprofits?

**Keith:** My first experience was with the United Way. I joined Peat Marwick and Mitchell—which is now KPMG—in Jackson, Mississippi, in 1963. After my first paycheck, they said I needed to sign up for the United Way contribution. I said, "United what?" I'd never heard of the United Way. Once that was explained, I began a payroll deduction plan for the United Way in 1963.

**Cagnetta:** Wow.

**Keith:** And I'm still there. I've been contributing, participating, and involved with United Way for over 60 years.

**Cagnetta:** A long time. That's incredible. So everywhere you've gone—

**Keith:** That commitment to United Way has followed me, along with other organizations, but United Way has been a flagship charitable organization for me. I know you have as well—you were chair yourself—and you've been chair multiple times.

**Cagnetta:** Yeah—Philadelphia, I know for sure, down here as well. And in other places?

**Keith:** I was involved with United Way everywhere I've lived—here, Baltimore, Philadelphia, and then back here again.

**Cagnetta:** Well, number one—thank you for your service to the United Way. I mean, it's been incredible.

**Keith:** It's been fun, and it's been rewarding, and I get to meet people like you.

**Cagnetta:** Well, I get to meet people like you as well. You've given a lot back to a lot of organizations over the years. You sit on a lot of boards.

**Keith:** Mhm.

**Cagnetta:** So you're still doing that as well?

**Keith:** No—I'm not on any boards now. I've stepped down and made room for a younger generation. But I have been active—I was on the United Way board with you at the time a few years ago, and I've been chairman of the community foundation, library foundation, and Museum of Discovery. I've done a lot of different things over the years, but most of my activity has been centered around downtown Fort Lauderdale.

**Cagnetta:** And you've been doing Howard County.

**Keith:** Yeah.
