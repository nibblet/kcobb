# The KPMG Journey (Interview)

_Transcript edited lightly for clarity and readability; wording and voice preserved._

## Full Text

**Keith:** I was an accounting major. I interviewed all of the Big Eight firms back then and got offers from most of them. I decided on KPMG because it was a better fit for me—and it turns out that was good judgment. I started in Jackson, Mississippi. I'd had a lot of opportunities; I picked that one because I thought it was the right fit.

**Cagnetta:** And you left Jackson at some point—you headed to Baltimore or Philadelphia?

**Keith:** First I was in Jackson eight years and became a partner in what is now KPMG. I moved to Orlando in 1970 and opened an office for the firm coincident with the opening of Disney World—I was there when they opened the park. We were there four years. Then I moved to Fort Lauderdale and ran the South Florida office for fourteen years, then Baltimore, Philadelphia.

**Keith:** I'm like a migrant—I move around.

**Cagnetta:** You moved around with the company—you were leading their offices.

**Keith:** I did.

**Cagnetta:** Reading your book, you don't sound like the typical accountant.

**Keith:** That's probably true.

**Cagnetta:** You're a leader—you have the capacity to help build teams.

**Keith:** I've worked on that over the years. I don't know if that's a natural talent—it's been a journey. I've had some wonderful people.

**Keith:** I think I've done a decent job organizing and running teams, but I've done an even better job recruiting people. When you have good people working for you, leadership is easier—they give you more to work with from the start.
