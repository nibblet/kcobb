## Full Text

Career Summary
KPMG was good to me. This terri ficfirm took me in, trained me and provided me with unparalleled opportunity to see the U.S.
business community from myriad perspectives. The firm imposed itself on me with the force of its rich history and professionalism. It molded me into a true professional, in every sense of the word. Year upon year, KPMG burnished my reputation and took me to places my wildest dreams had never imagined. In the end, it was indeed a lofty perch. I never asked for a raise. I never asked for a promotion.
I never even asked for an assignment. It was all handed to me on a platter. Rising above the hard work and considerable stresses over the years, is admiration for a venerable institution that took a boy out of the country to the pinnacle of American business. Along the way, I had the privilege of working with some incredible people —
the best of the best. In fact, two partners who worked for me went on to later become chairman and CEO of KPMG—Gene O’Kelly and
John Veihmeyer. I am honored and will be forever grateful for the generous experience provided by the firm.
My Alamo experience was truly unique. As I was leaving
KPMG, Chairman Jon Madonna remarked that he could not name another person who had left the accounting profession to assume such a lo ftyposition as CEO of a major company. I was indeed fortunate to have this opportunity. In a sense, however, I think I was bred for the job. I knew the company and its management well, and my breadth of knowledge and experience were in critical demand there. Moreover, I was unshackled. The company’s issues were not of my making; they were mine to solve. And I worked with blistering speed and decisiveness to attack them. Thankfully, it all worked out and we were successful. As with KPMG, I am deeply grateful for this opportunity.
I can think of no better way for someone to wrap up a successful career than to do what I have done for the past nearly twenty years.
I have reached into my personal reservoir of unique experience and been able apply some of my knowledge to corporate boardrooms,
consulting relationships and the university classroom. I have been extremely well compensated for some of these activities. Some have been not so financially lucrative, but I would have done many of them just for the enjoyable experience. It’s all been rewarding.
As I reflect on my career in its entirety, I am reminded of an excerpt from the lyrics of “ A Whole New World”, the song from
Aladdin that I used in that difficult presentation back in Orlando in
1993. In that song, Aladdin sings, “I can open your eyes, Take you wonder by wonder, Over, sideways and under, On a magic carpet ride. ” I truly feel as though I’vebeen on a magic carpet ride, and I’m truly grateful for the lift!

## Principles

- Acknowledge and be grateful for institutional development and mentorship.
- Take ownership of problems you inherit and treat them as yours to solve.
- Act decisively and swiftly when circumstances demand urgency.
- Leverage broad institutional experience and knowledge when stepping into leadership roles.
- Value the career-wide accumulation of experiences and give back via boards, consulting, and teaching.

## Decision heuristics

- When inheriting organizational problems, accept ownership and prioritize fast, decisive action to stabilize the situation.
- Use deep institutional knowledge and professional training as a primary resource when leading unfamiliar or struggling organizations.
- Maintain gratitude and recognize mentorship as part of leadership legacy and relationship-building.
