## Summary

A reflective chronology of a forty-plus-year business career emphasizing lifelong learning, mentorship, serendipity, and a fluid view of retirement.

## Full Text

How does one chronicle a professional career spanning forty-plus years? My story begins as I disrobed from a cap and gown following graduation ceremonies at the University of Southern Mississippi in 1963. I immediately went to work and began launching a long and fruitful career.

Narratives like this normally end with retirement, but in my case I’m not sure when that occurred—or whether it has occurred—or whether it will ever occur—or when it occurs in general. Retirement is a fuzzy concept for me. I’m still active in the business world, just in a different way and a smidgen less involved than in the past.

The following pages represent a collection of stories and events that occurred over various times and in various places. They are about my good fortunes and the many wonderful people who have shepherded me along the way. These stories are the backdrop to a lifelong learning process that has honed my skills and shaped my character. They also describe much of the serendipity that has accompanied me along the way.

Most of my stories are not dramatic. However, in the aggregate I am hoping they paint a picture of how a lone country boy from Mississippi was able to scramble around in a vast business world and find his way.

If there is a theme in this chronology, it’s one of gratitude. As I reflect on my career, my focus is transfixed by the numerous personalities that have influenced my life. I have had a lot of help along the way and people have been extremely generous in their tutelage and mentorship. For this I can only say, simply, that I am profoundly grateful.

## Principles

- Practice gratitude and acknowledge others' contributions.
- Cultivate and value mentorship and tutelage.
- Treat career as a lifelong learning process to hone skills and shape character.
- Remain adaptable about phases of involvement, including retirement.

## Decision heuristics

- When building a career, actively seek mentors and accept their tutelage.
- Treat professional development as ongoing: continually refine skills through experience and reflection.
- Stay open to serendipitous opportunities and adapt plans when unexpected events arise.
- Do not assume retirement is fixed; adjust involvement level to fit current interests and capacities.
